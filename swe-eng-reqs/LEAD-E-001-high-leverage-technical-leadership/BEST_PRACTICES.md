# LEAD-E-001 — High-leverage technical leadership

## Best practices
- Define leverage as measurable improvement in other teams' delivery, reliability, or decision quality—not personal ticket volume.
- Establish a baseline and measurement method before investing in a platform default, reusable pattern, or decision framework.
- Choose interventions that remove a repeated constraint for multiple consumers rather than solving one team's instance by hand.
- Make the expected engineering bar visible through concise examples, review criteria, and reusable artifacts.
- Preserve enough hands-on contact to test assumptions while deliberately transferring implementation and decision ownership.
- Re-measure adoption and team outcomes after rollout; retire interventions that create activity without multiplied output.

## Four habits
1. Begin each week by naming the team outcome to multiply.
2. Record a before metric and its measurement method before intervening.
3. Enable another engineer to own each important implementation or decision.
4. Review adoption and downstream outcomes before claiming leverage.
