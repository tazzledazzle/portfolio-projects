# ENG-E-022 — Bachelor's degree in CS, Engineering, or related technical field

## Best practices

- List the degree **exactly** as earned (field, institution, year) and mirror the posting phrasing: Bachelor's in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related technical field.
- If you used an alternate path, prepare an **explicit mapping**—but do not invent equivalency the posting does not grant.
- Pair the credential with **production proof**: degree alone is a gate; platform outcomes demonstrate capability.
- Keep diploma/transcript access ready for background checks without scrambling mid-process.
- Continuously learn beyond the degree (courses, certifications, shipped systems) and keep a short continuous-learning list.
- Treat education as foundation narrative: how coursework/projects connect to distributed systems, APIs, or developer tooling.

## Four habits

1. Keep credential documents ready and current.
2. Mirror posting education phrasing accurately in applications and resumes.
3. Pair education claims with production proof artifacts.
4. Do not overclaim equivalency the posting does not grant.
